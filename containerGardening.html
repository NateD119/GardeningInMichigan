Container Gardening

When it comes to Michigan, one thing that is consistant is that the seasons will always be here and they will always be changing. One of the ways that is not physically demanding is to switch out the plants in your containers seasonally. Container gardening lets you try new plants, new color combinations, and even non-native plants can thrive in containers. Most people that I talk to, think that container gardening is too hard or only looks good in the magazines. But it is simple, quick, fairly cheap and likely (needs to be native) able to take it right out of the container and straight into the garden to enjoy for years to come. You can also make friends really easily if your start offering them plants that have been living in your container, but needs to find a new home. Below I will be making a table that will go over a few options to combine together in the Spring, Summer, and Fall. I will include some photos of Winter containers, but they are a little more in depth that planting in warm Spring soil.
