Deer Resistant Plants

If you were to ask any greenthumb in the state of Michigan, I would bet that most have yearly issues with critters in the garden. From deer to rabbit to groundhogs, they each have their favorites plants to eat. Of the abovementioned pests, deer will come around 365 days a year if there is food available in your garden. In the cold winter months you may notice less and less green on any bushes not covered by snow, and that those same bushes may start of really slow or look like they are starting "fresh" each year. This is not only stressful for the plants, but it also removes the nodes that were originally ready to start growing at the first sign of warmth. So what is the easy solution? The only easy solution I have ever found to work is to completely take out all of your plants. So what else can be done? There are a myriad of different options, but foremost is picking plants that are deer resistant. Down below I have made a table that will list numerous plant names, if they require sun or shade, and what time of the season they bloom. Since we are not trying to attract pollinators here (of course we want them on these plants) but rather we need to know plants that are both sun or shade since deer do not differenciate between the two. Also remember, that ALL plants are deer resistant. It is just that the plants listed below and others like them are on the higher end of the resistance. So, when deer are hungry they will go after these plants last, deer will eat all plants if they are desperate enough.

Plants that are deer resistant
Plant Name Sun or Shade Bloom Time
Ajuga Full Sun to Part Shade 2-4 Weeks late June
Coneflower Full Sun 4-6 Weeks in July
Yarrow Full Sun 2-4 Weeks in June
Black Eyed Susans Full Sun 8-12 Weeks late June
Lupine Full Sun to part Shade 4-6 Weeks starting in June
Holly Full Sun to Part Shade Red Berries in Winter
Ferns Sun to Shade Attractive foliage all season
Coral Bells Sun to part Shade 2-4 weeks in Fall
A mixture of coneflowers and black eyed susans double as a deer resistant garden
A dahlia is risky, but well worth it
Peony's close to your house are fairly safe from deer
Lots of low woodland "green" is made up of various ferns
